Game Example

Positive Feedback in Minecraft

The variety of block material and tool selection gives the player infinite possibility to arrange each block in different locations. Each block or game item has a far-reaching linkage in between. In the beginning phase, the player could chop the tree to get wood. Then use wood; the player could chop it down to stick, wooden swords, wooden ax, and so many more items. Once the player gets the item, it increases the flighting capacity against the monster in the game and gives play opportunities and access to more maps and places.

Negative Feedback in Hollow Knight

On the other hand, the Negative Feedback stable the game and move to the foreseeable outcome. One of the great examples, also a great game, is Hollow Knight. While the player could do whatever they want and explore the map freely. The game mechanisms give a clear goal and let them discover. In the game, there is a mask that served as health points. The player needs to find several mask shards. The journey requires in-depth game engagement and reveals the storyline behind the game.

Positive Feedback in Overwatch Re Time

n the old version of Overwatch, there is a fixed amount of time heroes could respawn and back to battle. It stabilized the game, and always new heroes ran to the capture point cause the game took too long to end. It does not change the game result because as long as one opponent stands in capture point, the game will not end and stay in Overtime mode.

In the later update, the mechanism of respawning delay took online; there will time delay for defenders, generating more time for attackers. As positive feedback, it destabilized the game as some players had debates of fairness.

Conclusion 
This essay examined the cybernetics control theory in the settings of video game-related topics. The journey started inside the video game-like game sittings, gameplay, and game mechanics, then further move to how game design in hardware could influence the decision-making process in real-life. 
Video games are about a huge industry that the success behind it worth digging into. The cybernetics theory as mentioned chapters above seems a universal linkage for all the system has goals with intellectual property. The curation projects and the essay used several very well-known game examples like League of Legends, Overwatch, Hollow Knight, etc. For a very long time, they all are very popular games in their own category and stay top of the celling chart. All the examples I picked served as a shred of evidence about the gameplay need to have a perfect balance between positive and negative feedback to motivate players to continue playing. All the games give players a consistent and clear goal they could reach to. By keeping adding detail and information through a different feedback mechanism, the game became extremely engaging. 
Where is the future of feedback in the game system? Currently, we have visual, sound, haptic in a 2D sphere. The virtual reality system added another perspective to game development. With technology like Oculus hand tracking, or technology like eyeball tracking, there are more ways to engage with games; and it comes with more ways to provide feedback channels. I believe the cybernetics theory and the feedback scheme will stay very long and be a guide for all different elements in the gameplay design.
